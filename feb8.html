HARVEST, REWARD FOR SOWING

Written by: Ayomide Abraham

Wed. Feb. 8, 2017

Daily Reading: Eccl. 11:1-6 

 M.V: Gen. 8:22 
                         
While the earth remaineth, 
seedtime and harvest, and 
cold and heat, and summer 
and winter, and day and 
night shall not cease.

We are considering the reward for sowers in the kingdom today. We know from scriptures that harvest is the heritage of sowers. It is a law and an eternal principle in the word of God. In the same fashion that we found the revelation of how to pray, serve and how to handle obedience under the kingdom covenant reward system, we shall go deeper today to unveil other biblical truths that will help establish a sure harvest as we sow our seed.

For our seed to command the reward of a harvest we also need the discernment to locate a fertile ground for it. It is not every soil that is fertile according to Matthew 13. We have different kinds of soil. A seed that is sown on the stony ground cannot produce like the one which is sown on the good soil. Even for the seeds which are sown on the good soil, there are different levels of productivity.

Furthermore, the seed must be watered so that it will not die in the soil. The words which we speak after we have sown our seed are the means by which we water the seed. Poor talkers can jeopardize the fruitfulness of the seed they have planted (Proverbs 12:4 and 13:2). You donâ€™t go about speaking negatively or murmuring and complaining after you have sown the seed. It can be counterproductive.

Finally, you need to be patient until the harvest time. Apostle James pointed our attention to the practice of patience by farmers until the harvest season. Be wise as you sow your seed. Rejoice in the Lord.

Prayer Points

  1. I shall be a wise sower, in Jesus name.
  2. I shall not lose my harvest, in Jesus name.
