LOVE IS A GIVER

Sun. Feb. 12, 2017

Daily Reading: James 2:14-17 

 M.V: John 3:16 
                         
For God so loved
the world, that he
gave his only
begotten Son, that
whosoever
believeth in him
should not perish,
but have
everlasting
life
The work of faith is to show love; faith works by love (Galatians 5:6) and the proof of love is to give. Of all the attributes of God, love stands out. God’s love is always expressed in giving. God forgives us our sins because of His love for our souls. He protects us because of His love. When we reciprocate His love by responding to Him in love, He increases His benevolence in our lives. Hear Psalm 91:14 from the mouth of God: “Because he holds fast to me in love, I will deliver him, I will protect him, because He knows my name.” Read further the reward of loving Him in verses 15 and 16.

Love is a giver. It gives in kind, in caring, in material terms. Love gives through intercession and furthering the cause the weak. Job set a great example for us in Job 29:2-17. Please find time to read this passage. Job was a radical crusader for God in righteousness. He would not want anyone to be oppressed around him and he cared for those who were in need around him. Job did those things for which Jesus said He would reject some of His followers who failed to do according to Matthew 25:31-46. Love is the chief facilitator of rewards in the kingdom.

Love is not selfish. You cannot have the true love of Jesus Christ and be stingy. Jesus Christ gave everything in love-food, healing, support, hope, encouragement, raising the dead and more and when there was nothing else to give He gave Himself. There is no stopping a person who truly carries God’s nature of love from giving. When love is in your heart you will naturally want to help, to bring relief to the person in pain, food to the hungry and water to the thirsty, raiment to the naked and hope to the hopeless.

God’s love is redemptive in nature. It shows pity to the one who roams in sin because of ignorance; it beckons to the backslidden to return to his Maker. Love does not wish evil to anyone. Love relates both vertically and horizontally. Love does not discriminate. We hosted local bike riders who serve as transporters in our appreciation service two years ago and an unbeliever amongst them won the brand new bike in the raffle draw and I was very happy. He was shocked because he was a Muslim and was invited to the service by a friend. He wondered how he could go away with such a huge benefit from a church. That is God’s love. Rejoice in the Lord.

Prayer Points

  1. Thank You Jesus for Your undying love.
  2. I will never be tired of showing love, in Jesus name.
